All figures are in Indian Rupees (₹ Lakhs) unless stated otherwise.

## Thesis 
1. Saregama's core strategy revolves around acquiring music rights for a period of 60 years, with a strict internal discipline of only investing in music that has a payback period of 5 years or less. This creates a highly predictable, long-duration cash flow stream that behaves almost like an annuity.
2. Saregama was incorporated well before T-Series and as a result holds a massive and most authentic catalog of Hindi film music from the 1940s through the 1990s. These rights remain valid and will continue to generate royalties for decades. Crucially, this catalog cannot be replicated — no competitor can go back in time and acquire these songs. It is a permanently defensible asset
3. The company has begun exploring generative AI-based video creation, which could allow it to produce original visuals for songs at a fraction of the traditional cost and time. If successful, this would unlock an entirely new monetization layer on an already-owned asset base.
4. Saregama has strategically pivoted toward short-form video content, targeting platforms like Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts. Global content consumption, particularly in India, is growing at an extraordinary pace driven by cheap data and smartphone penetration. Generative AI video tools could further reduce production costs in this segment, improving margins significantly.
5. Platforms, Spotify and Gaana are the only two remaining that still operate a free tier. Every other platform has already moved to a fully paid model. Saregama currently earns 0.1 paisa per stream from free users and 0.5 paisa per stream from paid users on Spotify. As Spotify and Gaana follow the industry trend and go fully paid, every stream that was previously earning 0.1 paisa will shift to earning 0.5 paisa. No new content, no new deals, no additional effort — purely a volume re-rating on existing streams. Given the scale at which Saregama's catalog is streamed, this transition could be a meaningful step-up in revenue.

## Anti-Thesis
1.  Hardware sales are declining, product quality has slipped, and consumer audio has moved to smartphones. Carvaan still contributes to revenue today, but the business does not seems to be sustainable anymore.
2. Saregama consistently holds large idle cash balances earning sub-optimal bank returns instead of deploying into catalog acquisitions, AI capabilities, or buybacks. This meaningfully drags return on equity and is an underrated long-term concern.
